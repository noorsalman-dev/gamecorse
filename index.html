🛠️ Pro Tips for Step 3: The Animation Setup

When you use the AnimatedSprite2D node (which is likely what you meant for the visuals), there are two ways to bring in those frames:

Individual Frames: If your animation is a series of separate images, you can just drag them into the bottom "Animation Frames" window.

Sprite Sheets (The Grid Icon): Since most of those itch.io packs use sprite sheets, clicking the "Add Frames from Sprite Sheet" (the grid icon) is much faster.

Set the Horizontal and Vertical columns to match your sheet.

Select the frames you want (e.g., the first 5 for an Idle animation).

🏃 Next Step: Adding Movement

Once your animations are set, you'll want your character to actually move. Since you have a scripts folder ready, you can:

Right-click your CharacterBody2D node.

Select "Attach Script".

Save it in your /scripts folder.

Godot actually provides a "Basic Movement" template that is perfect for testing your new assets immediately!

📁 Organization Check

Your folder structure looks great. One small tip: inside your assets folder, you might want to create sub-folders like player, enemies, and environment. It keeps things clean when your project grows from a simple school project to a full game.
